WHAT IS IT!?!??!

A snake milker is someone who extracts, or 'milks', venom from poisonous snakes in order to create antivenom used by hospitals and laboratories. ... Snakes that are milked include cobras, mambas, vipers, asps, corals, copperheads, kraits, sea snakes and rattlesnakes.

WHERE DO YOU WORK!!!

 Snake milker who works in the field will find employment at a “serpentarium”. This is a type of lab that houses and researches snakes and other related venomous reptiles. The lab itself may also have other duties such as conservation, breeding, research, rehabilitation and veterinarian services.

ARE THERE ANY PREREQUISITES????!?!?!??!

Snake milking is a niche area but one with clear education path. High school students will need strong grades in math and biology. Chemistry will be helpful too to understand the chemical properties of the venom.
